Starters

Kicking off the excitement, here is chef's warm welcome to the table, openning of the show yet to reveal its beauty

    Ingedients

  • SCALLOPS
  • 6 Orkney scallops, hand-dived, in the shell
  • 25g of unsalted butter
  • 1 shallot, finely sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, finely sliced
  • 100ml of Noilly Prat vermouth
  • 250ml of fish stock
  • 100ml of double cream
  • salt

  • MORELS AND EDAMAME
  • 60g of frozen broad beans
  • 60g of morels, washed and halved
  • 1/2 garlic clove, chopped
  • 20g of unsalted butter
  • 12 small tarragon leaves
  • salt

  • SALT CRUST
  • 500g of plain flour
  • 275g of salt
  • 6 egg whites
  • 100ml of water, cold
Orkney scallops baked in the shell with morels & edamame
- by Martin Wishart

Method

  1. To make the salt crust, mix the flour and salt together in a large bowl and measure out 180ml egg white. Add the egg whites to the bowl and mix well
  2. Add the water, a little at a time, stirring until the mix forms a thick paste. Cover the salt crust in cling film and rest in the fridge for 1 hour before use
  3. Preheat the oven to 200˚C/gas mark 6
  4. For each scallop, slide a long thin-bladed knife along the inside of the flat part of the shell. The shell should now pull away
  5. Carefully lift the scallops out of the shell using a spoon. You will then need to scrape it against the shell to free it. Reserve both shells
  6. Remove the skirt and set aside. Cut away the orange roe and discard
  7. Rinse the white scallop meat in a little cold water and pat dry on a piece of kitchen towel
  8. Cut away and discard the black sac that is attached to the scallop skirt. Place the skirts into a bowl and leave them to rinse under a tap of running cold water for 15 minutes to remove any sand
  9. Boil the shells in water for 30 minutes to sterilize and then scrub them clean
  10. Melt the butter in a small saucepan, add the shallot and garlic and gently cook, without colouring, for a couple of minutes
  11. Add the Noilly Prat and boil until reduced to a glaze, then add the fish stock and boil until reduced by two thirds
  12. Add the cream and boil for a minute, then pass the sauce through a fine sieve, squeezing through all the sauce with the back of a spoon. Set aside to cool
  13. Cook the broad beans for a couple of minutes in salted boiling water, drain and set aside
  14. Sauté the morels and garlic in melted butter in a sauté pan over a medium heat until tender, then season with a little salt
  15. Lay the round, cupped scallop shells on the worktop and cut each of the scallops in half so you have two discs. Place two pieces in the centre of each shell
  16. Season the scallops with a little salt and place some of the morels and broad beans on top of each scallop, along with 2 tarragon leaves. Finish with 2-3 spoonfuls of the sauce
  17. Divide the salt crust into six balls and roll each one out to approximately 30cm
  18. Lay the flat shell on top of the prepared round scallop. Crimp the salt crust pastry around the shell to form an airtight seal
  19. Bake in the oven for 6 minutes (8 if they have come straight from the fridge)
  20. Open the shells with a sharp knife and serve at the table
