Mains

Long awaited moment - the heart of the menu and reveal of chef's soul

    Ingedients

  • COD
  • 1 side of Icelandic cod
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 1/3 handfuls of chives, chopped
  • 1000ml of extra virgin olive oil
  • salt

  • COD BRANDADE
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 80ml of extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to season
  • 270g of potatoes
  • 20g of fresh chives
  • 20g of fresh parsley
  • salt

  • AVOCADO RELISH
  • 2 avocados
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 150ml of water
  • 20ml of olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp fresh coriander
  • 1 pinch of cayenne pepper

  • CHORIZO CRISPS
  • 200g of chorizo sausage

  • SAUCE
  • 100g of squid
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 40ml of olive oil
  • 100g of Kalamata olives
  • 100g of chorizo sausage
  • 100g of roast red pepper
  • 100g of tomatoes
  • 15g of fresh parsley
  • 15g of fresh coriander
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced

    • Equipment

  • Fine sieve
  • Drum sieve
  • Baking parchment
Cod, avocado and chorizo
- by Agnar Sverrisson

Method

  1. Slice the cod loin into 100g portions, keeping 150g of the cod trimmings to make the brandade
  2. To make the cod brandade, lightly season the cod trimmings with 1 tsp salt, place in a small pan and cover with olive oil. Cook for 10-12 minutes
  3. Peel the potatoes, boil in salted water and then push through a drum sieve
  4. Place the warm mashed potato into a bowl and season with olive oil, salt and lemon juice. Use a spatula to mix together, trying not to overwork the mix
  5. Add the cod trimmings, julienned parsley and chopped chives and fold through the mash, trying not to break up the cod pieces
  6. To make the chorizo crisps, preheat the oven to 150-160°C/Gas mark 2-3
  7. Once cooked, transfer onto a paper towel-lined cold tray to drain off any excess fat
  8. To make the sauce, clean the squid and cut into long, thin strips. Cook with the salt in the olive oil over a low heat for 4-5 minutes
  9. When the squid is soft and tender, add the diced olives, chorizo and pepper
  10. Warm the sauce gently, trying not to overheat as this will ruin the squid
  11. Once warmed, add the diced tomato, julienned parsley, roughly chopped coriander and lemon juice and heat up so it is warm, but not too hot
  12. To make the avocado relish, add all of the ingredients into a blender and blend until a smooth purée forms. Pass the purée through a fine sieve and keep aside until needed
  13. Season the portioned cod well with salt, place into a hot oiled non-stick pan and cook over a high heat until golden brown and a nice crust has formed
  14. Preheat the oven to 180°C/Gas mark 4. Put the cod onto a baking tray and finish cooking in the oven for 2-3 minutes
  15. Once cooked, sprinkle with the chopped chives
  16. To serve, drip a large spoonful of avocado onto one side of the plate. Place a piece of cod in the centre with a spoonful of sauce; finally, spoon the cod brandade topped with a chorizo crisp or two to one side
